# Monetization Models

## Model Comparison

| Model | Revenue Source | Player Perception | Best For |
|-------|--------------|-------------------|----------|
| Premium | One-time purchase | Positive | Story games, indie |
| F2P + IAP | In-app purchases | Mixed | Mobile, live service |
| F2P + Ads | Ad revenue | Negative if intrusive | Hyper-casual |
| Subscription | Monthly fee | Positive if value clear | MMO, content-heavy |
| Battle Pass | Seasonal purchase | Positive if fair | Competitive, live service |
| Cosmetic-only | Skin/cosmetic sales | Very positive | Multiplayer |

## F2P Economy Design

### Currency Layers

```
Hard Currency (Gems) ← Real money purchase
  ↓ (conversion rate)
Soft Currency (Gold) ← Gameplay rewards
  ↓ (spending)
Items / Upgrades / Cosmetics
```

### Pricing Psychology

| Technique | Example | Why It Works |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| Anchor pricing | Show $9.99 pack next to $99.99 pack | Middle option feels reasonable |
| First purchase bonus | 2x gems on first buy | Lowers barrier to first spend |
| Limited time offers | "50% off, 23:59:59 remaining" | Urgency drives action |
| Bundle value | "Save 40% vs buying separately" | Perceived value |
| Starter pack | $0.99 for premium items | Converts free players cheaply |

### Ethical Monetization Principles

- ✅ Cosmetic-only purchases (no gameplay advantage)
- ✅ Battle pass with free track that's still rewarding
- ✅ Transparent odds for gacha/lootboxes
- ✅ Spending caps or parental controls
- ✅ All content achievable through gameplay (just slower)
- ❌ Pay-to-win mechanics
- ❌ Hidden odds
- ❌ Targeting vulnerable players (whales)
- ❌ Artificial energy systems that block play
- ❌ Lootboxes marketed to children

## Battle Pass Design

```
Season length: 8-12 weeks
Levels: 50-100
Time to complete: ~1 hour/day for casual, less for dedicated

Free Track:
  - Soft currency
  - Basic cosmetics
  - Gameplay items (enough to stay competitive)

Premium Track ($5-10):
  - Exclusive cosmetics
  - Premium currency (enough to buy next pass)
  - XP boosts
  - Emotes/effects

Key rule: Premium track should pay for itself if completed.
This creates a "subscription feel" — buy once, play to earn next one.
```

## Retention Mechanics

| Mechanic | Retention Type | Implementation |
|----------|---------------|----------------|
| Daily login rewards | Short-term | Escalating rewards over 7-30 days |
| Daily quests | Short-term | 3 easy tasks, 1 hard task |
| Weekly events | Medium-term | Limited-time game modes |
| Season pass | Long-term | 8-12 week progression |
| Guild/social | Long-term | Social obligation, team goals |
| Collection | Long-term | Completionism drive |
